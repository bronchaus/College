Career

Jonathan began his career as a junior designer at PrintRun. PrintRun is a small Irish graphic design firm that deals with many big named clients. Jonathan gained importance at his role and moved up in the company. After many years at PrintRun Jonathan decided to explore different avenues of graphic design, ultimately leaving the company. Two Squares was formed in 2011 and proved to be Jonathan's biggest challenge. The company took on a number of clients and pushed the boundaries of contemporary design and advertising.

Graphic Design

A big career move arrived when Jonathan decided to move to London. Opting to retain the clients acquired by Two Squares and take on some free lance work. Progressing his knowledge in the field of graphic communication, typography & marketing design. An established designer in his own right with many brands developed and clients acquired.

Date

Information

He is of strong temperament & is chiefly concerned with problem solving, attention to detail, good practice & quality workmanship. He creates exquisitely crafted contemporary typographic printed matter using an collection of English traditional wood and metal types in his Letterpress workshop.

Project

The project began as an induction in to a craft. Learning the equipment and tools required to create an individual type face. Using analog equipment and no computers in the workshop, The instruments used are all hand carved from wood and made from metal. Through a series of visits to Mr.Smith and his workshop, getting hands on with the equipment and learning spacing, fonts, font sizes and complimentary designs and layouts for print. Jonathan left the workshop with a catalogue of prints.

Times New Romans?

Date

Jan. 2013 - Nov. 2013

Information

Times New Romans? is a project Jonathan undertook at the beginning of 2013. The idea of the project was to question the beauty within type face and the literal ability of creating faces related to certain fonts.Times New Roman gets its name from the Times of London, the British newspaper. In 1929, the Times hired typographer Stanley Morison of Monotype, a British font foundry, to create a new text font.

Project

The project stemmed form the use 'face' in typeface... Jonathan can see the individual style and 'face' in each font. The idea was to incorporate and capture each subtle nuance for each font. The project Began with Times New Roman and the finished work is open to interpretation, However Jonathan feels that the image is pretty and clear. It depicts a certain biblical figure who had trouble with Romans of his own.
