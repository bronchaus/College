<h3><!-- InstanceBeginEditable name="SubTitle" -->What's New for 2016 / 2017 ?  <!-- InstanceEndEditable --></h3>
<!-- InstanceBeginEditable name="BodyText" -->
<p class="style2"><span class="style40">To view  minimum points for entry to Year 1 of our Undergraduate Programmes in 2015</span><a href="documents/CAORound1MinimumPointsAugust2015.pdf"> Click here </a></p>
<p class="style43">For more detailed information about points, see the <a href="http://www.cao.ie/index.php?page=points"> CAO website</a></p>
<p class="style43">See our <a href="documents/12789_FT_prospecuts_2016_17_web_001.pdf">2016/ 2017 Full Time Course Prospectus </a></p>
<p class="style43">For detailed information on the programme content of each course visit <a href="http://courses.itb.ie/">http://courses.itb.ie/</a></p>
<p class="style2">Our below Programmes have been renamed and restructured but their CAO Codes remain the same : </p>
<p class="style44"> BN025 Bachelor of Arts in Social and Community Development is now <strong>Bachelor of Arts in Community and Youth Development </strong></p>
<p class="style44"> BN115 Bachelor of Arts (Honours) in Social and Community Development is now <strong>Bachelor of Arts (Honours) in Community and Youth Development </strong></p>
<p class="style44"> BN001 Higher Certificate in Engineering in Electronics and Computer Engineering is now <strong>Higher Certificate in Engineering in Computer Engineering</strong> </p>
<p class="style44"> BN009 Bachelor of Engineering in Mechatronics is now <strong>Bachelor of Engineering in Mechatronic Engineering </strong></p>
<p class="style44"> BN121 Bachelor of Engineering (Honours) in Mechatronics is now <strong>Bachelor of Engineering (Honours) in Mechatronic Engineering </strong></p>
<p class="style44">&nbsp;</p>
<p><strong><span class="style31">It is  possible  to progress to NFQ Level 8 on all of our courses.</span></strong></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p class="text_green style21" style="margin-bottom: 0;"><strong>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;	</strong></p>
<p class="text_green style21" style="margin-top: 0;"><strong>FETAC's New Scoring System </strong></p>
<p style="margin-bottom: 0;"><span style="font-family:arial;color:rgb(51, 51, 51);">For entry into ITB,  all FETAC level 5 and level 6 awards are scored to deliver a maximum of 400 CAO points.</span></p>
<p>FETAC is currently undergoing a major restructuring and recoding systems of its awards so for the most current and up-to-date information please see <a href="../HowtoApply/fetac.html"><strong>www.itb.ie/fetac </strong></a></p>
<p class="style31"><strong>There is a  FETAC quota in place for entry on our Applied Social Studies in Social Care courses. </strong></p>
